4.1 Trends in the Data

Trends are common in economic time series (and not only). Observed over an extended period of time, gross domestic product, population, agricultural yields, and most recently global temperatures, all increase, on average. A trend is a pattern of change (increase or decrease) of a time series. It is possible for the direction as well as the rate of the change to alter over time. Interest rates, for example, were trending upward prior to 1980s, after which they started trending downward. Agricultural yields of cereal crops, for example, have been trending upward, but the slope of the trend has flattened over time.
